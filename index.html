About me (external UFS link)

Dr. Richard O. Ocaya is a National Research Foundation (NRF)-rated researcher. His main interests are computing, mathematical methods, new device characterization techniques, material science and microcontroller-based instrument design. He teaches in the University of the Free State, South Africa, in the Department of Physics. He collaborations with Saudi Arabia, Turkey, Japan, Egypt, South Korea, US. He specializes in electronics, solid-state physics and computational physics.

The purpose of this site
This site started out as part of Harvard University's wonderful Python and Javascript programming course that I enrolled into during the global coronavirus-driven (or COVID-19) pandemic. What better way to occupy the expansive national lockdown?

Having overcome the initial fumbling that produced a mediocre and rather pointless site, it occurred to me that the site could actually mirror what I do in real-life, to be a fully functional and pragmatic site that students especially can use.
